Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

1. Introduction

LunaSoft ("we", "us", or "our") develops and publishes LunaVPN (the "App"), a virtual private network (VPN) service that encrypts your internet connection and routes it through our servers. This Privacy Policy explains what information we do and do not collect, how the service works, and your rights regarding your data when you use LunaVPN.

LunaVPN operates a strict no-logs policy. We do not monitor, record, or store the websites you visit, the apps you use, your DNS queries, or the content of your traffic. We do not require an account, and we do not sell your data — ever.

2. Our No-Logs Commitment

A VPN works by carrying all of your internet traffic. Because of this, what we choose NOT to keep matters most. We do not log or store any of the following:

  • The websites, domains, or services you connect to.
  • Your browsing history or DNS query history.
  • The content of your network traffic.
  • Your original IP address paired with your activity.
  • Connection timestamps or session logs tied to your identity.

We may process limited, aggregated operational data (such as total server load or capacity) that is not tied to any individual user, solely to keep the service running reliably.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Information You Do Not Provide

LunaVPN does not require registration. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, photos, contacts, or precise location. You can use the App without giving us any personally identifiable information.

3.2 Data Stored Only on Your Device

Certain information is stored locally on your device and never sent to us. This includes your app preferences, your selected server, and — for non-subscribers — the amount of free trial time you have used. This data stays on your device and is removed if you uninstall the App.

3.3 Purchases and Subscriptions

Subscriptions are processed entirely by the Apple App Store. We do not receive or store your payment information, credit card numbers, or billing address. We only receive a confirmation from Apple of whether you have an active subscription, so the App can unlock premium access on your device.

3.4 Diagnostics

If you choose to share crash and usage diagnostics with developers through your device settings, Apple may provide us with anonymized, aggregated reports that help us fix bugs. These reports do not contain your browsing activity or identify you personally.

4. How We Use Information

The limited information involved is used only to:

  • Establish and maintain your encrypted VPN connection.
  • Unlock premium features for active subscribers.
  • Operate, maintain, and improve the reliability of the service.
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations.

We do not sell, rent, or trade any user data. We do not use your data for advertising, and we do not build profiles of your online activity.

5. DNS and Third-Party Services

To deliver the App and resolve domain names while connected, we rely on the following providers:

  • Apple App Store (iOS) — apple.com/legal/privacy
  • Cloudflare (DNS resolver, 1.1.1.1) — cloudflare.com/privacypolicy
  • Server hosting providers that run our VPN servers under their own data-protection terms.

These providers process data under their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review them.

6. Data Security

Your VPN connection is protected with strong, modern encryption, and traffic between the App and our servers is encrypted end-to-end at the tunnel level. We follow industry-standard practices to protect data in transit. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Children's Privacy

LunaVPN provides unrestricted access to the internet and is intended for users aged 17 and older. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided information to us, please contact us and we will take steps to address it.

8. Your Rights

Because we do not store personally identifiable user data on our servers, there is generally no personal data for us to access, correct, or delete. Depending on your jurisdiction (such as the EU under GDPR or California under CCPA), you may have the following rights:

  • The right to access any data we may hold about you.
  • The right to request deletion of any data.
  • The right to object to processing.
  • The right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email address below.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the most recent changes were made. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
